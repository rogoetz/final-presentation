what is XKCD?

XKCD is a webcomic created by artist Randall Munroe. He started it in 2005, as a place where he could put up old doodles he had scanned, but has since become something else, with original content being posted three times a week.

How does a webcomic relate to this class?

"in many ways, this entire class is designed to help you understand the jokes in XKCD" --John D. Martin III

Explain XKCD

XKCD is a webcomic that, according to the author, is about 'romance, sarcasm, math, and language'. The author is a computer science geek who worked at NASA prior to starting XKCD, so many of his jokes related to computer concepts, some of which we've learned in class.

What kind of person makes this sort of thing?

Randall Munroe does

As mentioned, he used to work at NASA as a roboticist prior to starting XKCD. He now works only on XKCD and some related projects.

Personal and Oddball

A lot of the comic is jokes about science and technology, or small, silly narratives about various characters, but it also gets personal sometimes. This comic is looking back at the author's wife's battle with breast cancer, something he made several comics about.

Other Projects

What If

As mentioned, there are some other projects that Munroe has worked on. He had a blog and published a book based on it, answering absurd questions with hard science. Examples include 'how much power can Yoda output' and 'what would a mole of moles look like'. He also published a book called 'Thing Explainer', which explains difficult concepts like space travel using only the 1,000 most common English words.

Is this actually a thing?

2014: Best Graphic Story Time

In case any of you were wondering why I was blathering on about a random webcomic, it actually is quite well-known. Munroe won a Hugo Award for one comic, and several others have been widely used and shared. He also had an Asteroid named after him by the International Astonomical Union.
